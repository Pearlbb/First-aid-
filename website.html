    FIRST AID

    First aid is the immediate life saving measure given to a person suffering from a minor or seriousinjury or illness before the arrival of the doctor or any medical personnel

    some common emergencies and their first aids
    1 Animal Bites e.g dog and cat
  • Wash the wound with mild soap and water
  • if the wound is bleeding, apply pressure using a clean towel
  • apply anti biotic ointment on the injury
  • keep the wound elevated above the heart to prevent swelling
  • catch the animal if possible
  • reassure the victim and help him/her meet a doctor
    • 2 snake bites
  • remove jewelry or watches from area of the bite
  • keep the area of the bite below the heart to slow the spread of the venom
  • keep the person calm and at rest
  • use a razor or a sharp knife and cut into the fang mark to let blood flow out. if possible, suck the venom with your mouth and spit it out
  • cover the bite with a clean loose fitting bandage
  • make sure the victim does not move to avoid spread of the venom
  • tie a cloth above the bite, fairly firm but not too tight to avoid spread of the venom
  • if possible, kill the snake and take it to the doctor
    • 3 insect bites eg scorpion sting
  • tie a piece of rope fairly above the sting to prevent the spread of venom in blood
  • use twizzers to remove the sting from the body
  • place ice on the sting
  • clean the wound with mild soap and water and apply balm
  • give pain killer to reduce pain
    • 4 epelepsy
  • remove all hard or sharp objects around the victim
  • don't put anything in the mouth of the victim, he or she cannot swallow or bite his tongue
  • place a soft material under the head and loosen any tight clothing
  • do not give the victim anything to eat or drink untill he is fully recovered
  • gently place the victim on his sight to keep the the airways clear when he recovers
  • stay beside the victim untill he regains unconsciousness

    • it is a common practice for people to run away in all directions when a person is having a fit.it is not infectious and cannot be caught.running away will not help the patient. we should rather try to help the patient as explained above

    5 nose Bleeding
  • let the victim seat down upright and remain calm
  • let him lean slightly forward
  • drop the head slightly forward
  • pinch the nostrils below the bridge of the nose and apply direct pressure with the thumb and index fingers for atleast 10 minutes to stop the flow of blood
  • spit out any blood in the mouth
  • encourage the victim to breath through his/her mouth while pinching the nostrils
    • 6 Fainting
  • let the patient sit or lie down with the head lower than the knee
  • loosen any tight clothing
  • on recovery, sips of water should be given
    • 7 stroke
  • remove clothing and immediately cool the body using ice blocks or sprinkle water
  • massage arms and legs to assist in blood circulation
  • if consciousness is regained,give the person cold drinks containing salt. else, contact the doctor
    • 8 drowning
  • use rope or long stick to pull out the victim
  • turn the victim to face downward
  • use a clean cloth over a finger and clear the mouth off any mud, or weed
  • loosen any tight clothing found around the neck and waist and carry out artificial respiration
